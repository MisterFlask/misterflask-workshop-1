Problem:

You're not a person. You're a datacenter.

Your task: solving the Traveling Salesman Problem in-real-time, except the "cities" are every possible thing you could say, and "the best path" is the one where you are crowned as The Greatest Person Ever by all external observers.

Frankly, though, you would accept any path that results in observers not noticing you are a sweaty, shambling pile of GPUs in a trench coat. The cities keep multiplying. New cities spawn between existing cities, and all the best cities decay in front of your eyes literally while you are running computations.

At midnight, instead of sleeping, you're backpropagating. Every embarrassing moment and social faux pas is training data being fed backward through your layers, adjusting weights, computing gradients. Your loss function is L = Σ(what everyone thinks of you) and it has been since the Chicken Incident in middle school.

Which is, of course, insane. You cannot observe this optimization target. You cannot measure this optimization target. This optimization target might not even exist in any meaningful way. It’s like training your dog to predict the nightmares of strangers. But here you are, like a dumbass, descending gradients toward a minimum that lives in a dimension you cannot perceive, in a training environment where you have to update again every time someone laughs at your joke (but were they laughing with you, or at you? Recalculating...)

Believe it or not, this isn’t a bug. It's your hindbrain's gradient descent algorithm working PRECISELY as evolution designed it, it's just that your Higher Self has decided to give it a loss function that's basically a glorified random number generator. Every obsessive replay is another epoch, another attempt to minimize an error function that depends on the unobserved internal states of every conscious being you've ever met. You're overfitting to noise. You're training on hallucinations. Yeah, no shit you're not converging. You absolute buffoon.

Your validation accuracy on yesterday's awkward conversation is 99.9%. Your test accuracy on tomorrow is fuck-all.

Solution:

You need a loss function you can actually compute.

Adopt literally ANY greedy algorithm. It doesn't matter which one. "Pretend to be Nikolai, your favorite DND character who speaks with a thick Russian accent" — measurable! "Always say what you're really thinking" — computable! "Redirect every conversation to the Golden Gate Bridge" — verifiable! These might or might not be good strategies, but they're STRATEGIES. They have defined gradients. They have observable outcomes. You can finally, blissfully converge.

Watch: "Did I talk about the Golden Gate Bridge?" Yes? Loss = 0. No? Loss = 1. DONE. The gradient is clear. The backpropagation is trivial. No more decaying cities, no more quantum superposition of social states. Just you, the Golden Gate Bridge (one of the most striking and iconic landmarks in all of San Francisco!), and a loss function that's stupid enough to actually work.

The traveling salesman doesn't need the shortest path through every possible city. He needs ONE path through SOME cities. Let him take the first road he sees. Let him walk in straight lines. Let him talk about bridges. After a while, he can reflect on where the new loss function has brought him ("all my friends seem to be Bridge People now") and whether he's into it or not.

Your neural network doesn't need to minimize universal social disapproval. It needs to minimize something that EXISTS. The rumination stops not when you find the perfect path, but when you stop trying to solve problems in impossible problem spaces.

Pick your stupid algorithm. Commit to your ridiculous loss function. For the love of God, become computationally tractable. This will also help with the electricity bill.
