Problem

There's a leather bag in your chest cavity, right below your heart.

Every man has one, as it turns out. Inside: a special type of coin, each one double-sided. Heads shows a golden emperor, crown gleaming, scepter raised, a confident smile gracing his handsome features. Inscribed on this side are the words: “I have unilaterally made a decision about our shared reality.” Tails shows the same emperor, but his face is a skull, his crown is barbed wire, his scepter drips something dark. Inscribed on this side, identically, are the words: “I have unilaterally made a decision about our shared reality.”

Countless such coins litter the world; you pass them every day. Pristine and beautiful and innumerable, each one slightly different from the last. Out of the corner of your eye, they beckon with possibility. Mostly you ignore them, as do most decent folk.

Only cowards take none, of course. They leave their bag untouched, empty, safe. They move through the world like ghosts; never offensive, never memorable, never anything. They die reasonably well-liked, having disturbed no one.

Other people grab handfuls, stuff their pockets, swallow coins whole until they jangle in their throats. They soon take the form of the True Emperor: an unspeakable beast with seven maws, each dripping with ichor. They are loved, hated, despised, worshipped. They leave scars on everyone they touch.

It is a regrettable fact about the world that becoming the True Emperor works, in a sense. The True Emperor gets many things (including, occasionally, jail time); the ghost gets nothing.

Any man could become this if he wanted. He could, tomorrow, snatch each and every coin he across and jam each into his mouth, cram his stomach with coins to the point of bursting, and before the day was out he too would take on the aspect of the True Emperor.

You take a coin every week, or thereabouts. Too few. You know it's too few; everyone around you also knows it's too few, but generally it's considered indecorous to mention this fact. Twitter seems to advocate that one should take only the head side of the coin without the tail side, and reacts with revulsion and rage to any suggestions that this might be impossible. Meanwhile, romance novel covers often feature full-on drooling maws and blackened carapaces. (As it happens, people generally do like ghosts, but ghosts do not grace the front of romance novels.)

Late at night, you feel the absence in your mostly-empty bag. The whisper comes:

Take more, you fucking coward.

Solution

You need rules. Real, honest-to-god rules. Considered at length, in private, written and enforced by yourself alone. Decide what coins you will and will not take. How much gravity you're entitled to exert on a room or a person, and what unilateral actions you are willing to stand behind taking.

Then, never take more coins than your rules require, and (for many this is the hard part) never take less.

When someone recoils from the hint of ichor on your canines, when they see a monster looking back at them, you accept it in the moment. You don't drop coins at their feet. You don't shrink back to ghost-level. Their fear doesn't override your considered judgment about how many coins should be allowed to exist within your bag. Much later, in private, you examine: Were my rules right and reasonable, here?

The True Emperor isn't someone who took too many coins one time; it's someone who lacks the inclination to ever not take a coin. The ghost isn't someone who took too few; it's someone who at one point had coins and then dropped them in the face of the last person who had opinions about their existence, then was afraid of picking any up ever again.

The bag will always whisper that you could have taken more. Some people will see the True Emperor in your shadow no matter how few coins you carry. Other people will see a ghost no matter how many.

Ultimately, you must make peace with both of these facts and exist regardless.

It is what it is.
