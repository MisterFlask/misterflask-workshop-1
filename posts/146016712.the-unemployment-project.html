I think a reasonable definition of “burnout” for a white-collar employee is this:

I used to be able to make myself care about this job enough to do it at least reasonably well. This is no longer true; I have an abstract sense that I ought to care about it but on a pretty fundamental level no longer do.

Anyway, that’s about where I am at Microsoft! So now I’m looking into alternative options.

The job market right now is unappealing at best, but on the other hand, I’m in a place in my life where I have, conservatively, several years of living expenses saved up (or maybe just like 4 if I don’t feel like touching my retirement accounts.) So I’m going to quit once I’m back from vacation, I think.

But “unemployment for unemployment’s sake” isn’t really useful. In order for me to consider this unemployment break “successful” I want to have gotten something out of it.

What do I want to get out of it? Well,

(1) I want to get swole! Beefy! Jacked! Mostly for, y’know, shallow aesthetic reasons.

(2) I want to make a lot of friends and chat with a lot of people in my apartment complex and elsewhere. I think the standard human behavior in cities is to cloister ourselves off into preexisting friend groups; I think this impulse is bad and ought to be fought. Relatedly:

(3) I want to effectively subdue my social anxieties around “speaking to people I do not have social permission to speak to.” This is useful for both friend-making and for dating; the guys I know who are most successful in dating are people that aggressively make a point of speaking to everyone. (I also have a cousin, Annie, who is an absolute legend at chatting up strangers.)

(4) I’d like to pivot my professional focus to LLMs and, if i’m able, mechanistic interpretability. I’ve heard of a lot of resources for doing this but prior to now haven’t really had the time or energy to really engage with them. It helps that I met a guy recently with basically similar objectives to mine; I’d like to see if I can’t get him on board with doing a joint project.

(5) I want to grow the Minneapolis-St Paul ACX meetup group to where it can sustain itself without my direct input. I think this is feasible though it’s definitely not there yet.

(6) I want to get a lot more active in both local friend groups I’m not yet engaged with (friends-of-friends)

(6a) …and I also want to be a lot more engaged in the broader worldwide rationalist community. What a great bunch of nerds.

(7) I want to run a blog under my real name; probably we’re talking a mix of technical posts (stonks and drug studies being my areas of interest) and personal posts (especially around my adventures in social anxiety and dating.)

Honestly if I can say that even two of these are “done” in any sense once I finish my unemployment adventure, I’ll have considered it a success and be thrilled with the overall outcome.

This isn’t as well fleshed-out as I’d like but it’s better than nothing!
