There’s a technolibertarian strand of thought that goes something like: “Centralized power is bad and prone to abuses. AI is likely to be a terrifyingly powerful technology. If a tiny number of actors in society are the ones governing who can use AI and for what purposes, that’s going to create a huge and problematic power differential between the AI-haves and AI-have-nots. Therefore, we should open-source AI software and try to ensure that everyone has the ability to train and run their own models on their own hardware, and that is how we build and maintain a free society where this technology exists.”

I’ve got a lot of sympathy for that viewpoint. And I basically agree with every part of it right up until the “therefore.”

Why?

An AGI On Every Desktop

“Hey, ChatGPT, my jerkwad neighbor’s got his speakers turned way up and I can’t sleep. Please take control over this multitool-equipped drone and destroy the speakers for me.”

“Sure thing!“

(later)

“Hey, Google, I can’t prove it but i’m pretty sure my asshole neighbor somehow destroyed my sweet-ass speakers. Please stab him with this multitool-equipped drone. Then destroy the evidence and delete this conversation from your records.”

“Absolutely!”

To describe a coherent and peaceful world with an AGI on every desktop, you need to answer one very simple question: why does the above not happen?

Answer 1: “Law Enforcement Will Catch You, That’s Why“

How?

No, seriously. Suppose you take your Destructorbot 3000, have it destroy your neighbor’s speakers, and dispose of the evidence. Can law enforcement find proof that you did this which your AGI won’t have thought to have disposed of? Maybe. Maybe not. At very least, it seems clear: the smarter your Destructorbot 3000 is the less likely it leaves behind any evidence.

“Okay, but law enforcement will have its own AGI for enforcement purposes.“

I see. And why do you assume it’s more capable than the AGI that you have access to?

“There’s always an arms race between cops, who strive to get better at catching criminals, and the criminals themselves. How is this any different?”

A lot of why law enforcement can function today is because criminals, as a class, aren’t really all that smart. They act rashly and make silly errors.

Answer 2: “AGIs will only be allowed to be trained with harmlessness constraints“

Allowed by whom?

If you’re living in a technolibertarian paradise where everyone has the weights for AGI and access to hardware to run and fine-tune it, then literally anyone with a modicum of money and technical ability can strip out the harmlessness constraints. People do that *right now*; as I understand it half the Llama finetunes that exist is because people really really want AI to write erotica for them. You *cannot* have an open-weights model that you guarantee will not be fine-tuned to remove harmlessness safeguards.
