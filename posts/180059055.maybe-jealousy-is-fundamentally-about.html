A thing I’ve noticed about myself is that the times I get fiercely jealous is when (1) I’m not getting my needs met, and (2) I notice that a possible explanation for those needs not getting met is that another guy has gotten in my way. Like: if I’ve shot my shot and a crush isn’t interested in me, I’m a bit bummed out but otherwise okay, but if she then turns around and becomes interested in someone else, the base lizard-brain part of me immediately starts wanting to brain my romantic rival with a rock. Do I endorse this? No, I’m sure he is a lovely person. And yet.

I always chalked this up to standard-issue romantic jealousy. Typical human experience. Which is why I’ve always done the monogamy thing (also it’s the standard relationship script in my area, which makes it much easier to date.)

But at Slutcon specifically, I got a very interesting experience: having a bunch of varyingly-intimate micro-relationships with a bunch of different pretty girls all at the same time. And during those relationships in each case I got to witness them doing stuff with other guys while I was off wandering the venue. One girl I had a brief, fun flirtation with on day 1 and then, on almost every other occasion that I saw her, she was with another guy and therefore unavailable.

And it was… fine, actually? I quite liked many of these girls; there were mild but real crush-shaped emotions there, not unlike a superficial crush IRL. But the jealousy around seeing them with other dudes was absent.

It was probably not coincidental that romantic attention did not feel scarce in this environment. It felt like a place where I could simply stroll around being myself and cuddles and flirting would just happen.

Which makes me wonder: maybe the core experience of jealousy for me is specifically downstream of feeling like I’m not getting my needs met romantically and another guy is standing in my way. If I feel secure and in abundance: no jealousy.

I wonder if this is similar to what, in a different much-poorer society, might be called “food jealousy.” Where I become enamored of, I dunno, a really good cut of steak and then being devastated/filled with rage when it’s consumed by another guy, because I’m hungry and my opportunities to sate that hunger are scarce. And that the reason we don’t have a word for “food jealousy” in our culture is because we are so laden with cheap calories that the idea of widespread famine is unthinkable going back generations, and “someone getting food that was supposed to be yours” is a niche enough situation where we don’t need to have a specific word for it.

I feel like a lot of the “YOU’RE A CUCK” responses targeted to the men in polyamorous relationships exist because the assumed experience of the man in those relationships is “the girl is having sex with a number of other partners; the man is sitting at home alone, feeling deprived and lonesome.” I think this speaks to a very real fear of men: of feeling locked in a relationship where they are no longer getting their physical/emotional needs met, because another guy is sucking up all the time and attention that would ordinarily go into getting those needs met.

And when I read the blogs of people who seem to be in successful polyamorous relationships (see also: the Aella tweet above), it is generally the case that they describe both the man and the woman as either functionally 98% monogamous, or (as in the tweet) they are both having lots of satisfying dalliances with other people. Probably not a universal, but it feels like it ought to be true.

If this is true, it probably follows that a prerequisite to a polyamorous relationship being successful— at least for me and people shaped like me— is for both the partners to be reasonably successful in establishing dates and/or relationships outside the primary partnership, rather than just one partner being successful at this. Otherwise the partner who’s not successful here will start feeling In Lack instead of In Abundance and jealousy naturally results.

Anyway, this whole experience and line of reasoning has made me rather more interested in trying the polyamory relationship model, since it’s a plausible reason for suspecting that it won’t result in an inevitable emotional implosion. (It helps that my Slutcon experience has reminded me that I’m actually quite rizzful when I feel as though my flirting isn’t a Declaration Of Likely Monogamous Attachment.)

Probably, yeah. I would not say I’m really attempting to “contribute to the discourse” as much as parse out my own feelings around this stuff for self-interested reasons.

One weekend at a sex convention is pretty thin evidence for you Being Polyamorous.

Sure is!

Ultimately, most life decisions are based off of thin evidence and deeply imperfect information. And a thing I’ve noticed in watching the relationships of friends and acquaintances is noticing that… well, expecting someone to be able to serve all of your romance-adjacent needs forever is a tall order, and monogamy basically says “if this stops being true, either the relationship has to end or you have to do without forever.”

I dislike this, and polyamory seems like a plausible way of addressing this core issue, where the most apparent downside (jealousy) seems basically manageable.
