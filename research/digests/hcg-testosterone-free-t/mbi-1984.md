# Digest: SHBG Changes with Testosterone vs HCG Treatment

Source: [mbi-1984](../../sources/hcg-testosterone-free-t/mbi-1984.md)
Access: abstract
Last Updated: 2026-01-16

## Summary

Classic study demonstrating that testosterone and HCG produce divergent effects on SHBG despite both increasing free testosterone. Exogenous testosterone decreases SHBG (increasing free T fraction), while HCG either maintains or increases SHBG. This has significant implications for interpreting total vs free testosterone levels.

## Key Findings

**Free Testosterone Increases (both treatments effective):**
| Group | Treatment | Baseline | Post-Treatment |
|-------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Klinefelter's | T enanthate | 94 ± 20 pg/mL | 271 ± 50 pg/mL |
| HH patients | HCG | 14 ± 5 pg/mL | 276 ± 41 pg/mL |
| Normal men | T enanthate | 211 ± 52 pg/mL | 390 ± 83 pg/mL |
| Normal men | HCG | 220 ± 37 pg/mL | 330 ± 90 pg/mL |

**SHBG Response (KEY FINDING - divergent):**
| Group | Treatment | Baseline SHBG | Post-Treatment SHBG |
|-------|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Normal men | T enanthate | 6.5 ± 1.2 | 4.3 ± 0.4 ↓ |
| Klinefelter's | T enanthate | 16.4 ± 2.0 | 4.3 ± 0.5 ↓ |
| HH patients | HCG | - | Unchanged |
| Normal men | HCG | 6.6 ± 0.7 | 8.6 ± 1.0 ↑ |

**Clinical implication**: At equivalent total testosterone levels, exogenous testosterone produces higher free T (due to SHBG suppression), while HCG may produce relatively lower free T (SHBG unchanged/increased).

## Operationalization of Key Concepts

| Concept | How Measured/Operationalized | Notes |
|---------|------------------------------|-------|
| Free testosterone | Direct measurement (pg/mL) | Gold standard for bioactive T |
| SHBG | DHT binding capacity (ng DHT bound/mL) | Older methodology but valid |
| Total testosterone | Serum T measurement | Standard assay |
| Treatment duration | 3 months | Adequate for steady-state |

**Operationalization concerns**: SHBG measured as DHT binding capacity rather than direct SHBG concentration - this is an older methodology but functionally equivalent.

## Methodology

- **Study Type**: Prospective intervention study (non-randomized)
- **Sample**: n=20 total (5 per group × 4 groups)
- **Groups**:
  - Normal men + T enanthate
  - Klinefelter's + T enanthate
  - Normal men + HCG
  - HH patients + HCG
- **Duration**: 3 months
- **Doses**: T enanthate 200 mg IM q2weeks; HCG 2000 IU 3×/week

## Methodological Critique

### Strengths
- Direct free testosterone measurement
- Comparison across multiple patient populations
- Adequate treatment duration for steady-state
- Both treatments elevated free T significantly

### Limitations
- Small sample size (n=5 per group)
- Non-randomized design
- Different patient populations received different treatments (Klinefelter's got T, HH got HCG)
- Did not control for baseline SHBG differences
- Mechanism for differential SHBG response not explained

## Relevance to Research Question

**Directly addresses free testosterone question**: This study shows that both HCG and exogenous testosterone increase free testosterone, but through different mechanisms:
- **Exogenous T**: Directly adds testosterone AND suppresses SHBG → amplified free T
- **HCG**: Stimulates endogenous T production but SHBG unchanged/increased → proportionally less free T per unit total T

**Clinical consideration**: When comparing total testosterone levels between HCG and TRT patients, the free testosterone may differ significantly due to SHBG effects. HCG patients may need higher total T to achieve equivalent free T.

## Connections to Other Sources

- Mechanistically distinct from coviello-2005 (which focused on ITT rather than SHBG)
- Important context for interpreting hsieh-2013 free testosterone data
- Explains why some HCG monotherapy patients may have good total T but persistent symptoms (if free T inadequate)
