# Digest: Concurrent TRT + HCG for Fertility Preservation

Source: [hsieh-2013](../../sources/hcg-testosterone-free-t/hsieh-2013.md)
Access: abstract
Last Updated: 2026-01-16

## Summary

Clinical study demonstrating that concurrent low-dose HCG (500 IU EOD) with testosterone replacement therapy preserves spermatogenesis in hypogonadal men. No patients became azoospermic, and 35% achieved pregnancy with their partners during follow-up.

## Key Findings

**Testosterone Response:**
- Total testosterone: 207.2 → 1,055.5 ng/dL (p<0.0001)
- Free testosterone: 8.1 → 20.4 pg/mL (p=0.02)
- Substantial increases in both total and free T

**Semen Parameters (maintained):**
| Parameter | Baseline | Follow-up |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Volume | 2.9 mL | Maintained |
| Density | 35.2 million/mL | Maintained |
| Motility | 49.0% | Maintained |
| Forward progression | 2.3 | Maintained |

- No significant differences in semen parameters over >1 year follow-up
- **Zero patients became azoospermic**
- No impact from testosterone formulation (gel vs injection)

**Pregnancy Outcomes:**
- 9/26 (35%) men contributed to pregnancy during follow-up
- Demonstrates real-world fertility preservation

## Operationalization of Key Concepts

| Concept | How Measured/Operationalized | Notes |
|---------|------------------------------|-------|
| Spermatogenesis preservation | Serial semen analyses (volume, density, motility, progression) | Standard WHO criteria |
| Testosterone response | Serum total and free testosterone | Standard clinical labs |
| Fertility | Partner pregnancy during follow-up | Real-world outcome |
| HCG dosing | 500 IU IM every other day | Fixed protocol |

**Operationalization concerns**: Retrospective design; semen analysis timing may vary; partner fertility factors not controlled.

## Methodology

- **Study Type**: Retrospective cohort study
- **Sample**: n=26 hypogonadal men
- **Mean age**: 35.9 years
- **Follow-up**: Average 6.2 months (some >1 year)
- **Protocol**: TRT (topical gel or weekly IM injection) + 500 IU HCG IM every other day

## Methodological Critique

### Strengths
- Real-world clinical data
- Multiple outcome measures (semen, hormones, pregnancy)
- Adequate sample size for clinical pilot
- Pregnancy outcome confirms functional fertility

### Limitations
- Retrospective design
- No control group (TRT alone)
- Variable follow-up duration
- Different TRT formulations (gel vs injection)
- Partner fertility factors not controlled

## Relevance to Research Question

**Free testosterone data directly relevant**: This study provides real-world data on free testosterone response to concurrent HCG+TRT:
- Free T increased 2.5× (8.1 → 20.4 pg/mL)
- Total T increased 5× (207 → 1,056 ng/dL)

**Important clinical context**: While mbi-1984 showed HCG alone doesn't suppress SHBG, this concurrent therapy study shows that the combination still produces excellent free T levels, likely because the exogenous testosterone component suppresses SHBG while HCG maintains testicular function.
