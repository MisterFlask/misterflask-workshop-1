# Digest: Selles et al. 2021 — Self-Focus and Safety Behaviors in Adolescent Social Anxiety

Source: [selles-2021](../../sources/safety-behaviors-social-anxiety-exposure/selles-2021.md)
Access: full-text (open access)
Last Updated: 2026-03-14

## Summary

First experimental manipulation of self-focused attention and safety behaviors in adolescents (ages 11–14). Within-subjects design: two conversations, one "with" self-focus and SBs, one "without." Compared high (n=28) vs. low (n=29) socially anxious adolescents. Adapted McManus et al. (2008) adult paradigm for younger population. Used Adolescent Social Behaviour Questionnaire (ASBQ).

## Key Findings

- Self-focus + SBs increased anxiety feelings and appearance ratings across both groups
- High-anxious adolescents reported significantly higher habitual SB use (M=1.42) vs. low-anxious (M=0.74) on ASBQ
- "With" condition produced more critical partner ratings and more negative observer ratings
- Experimental manipulation produced detrimental effects on conversation quality

## Operationalization of Key Concepts

| Concept | How Measured/Operationalized | Notes |
|---------|------------------------------|-------|
| "With" SBs condition | Written instruction: "Focus on yourself and watch yourself during this conversation. Think about how you are coming across to the other person... be careful about what you say, deciding whether it is good enough to say before you say it." | Combines SBs + self-focus in single instruction |
| "Without" SBs condition | Written instruction: "Focus on the other person. Don't think about yourself or how you're coming across. Just be your natural self... Say whatever comes to mind. Absorb yourself in the conversation." | Also combines SB absence with external focus instruction |
| Safety behaviors targeted | Self-monitoring of appearance, rehearsing responses before speaking, filtering statements before saying them | "One particular set" — not exhaustive |
| Habitual SB use | Adolescent Social Behaviour Questionnaire (ASBQ): 29 items, 0-3 frequency scale, Cronbach's α = 0.74 | Adapted from adult SBQ |
| Compliance | 0-100 visual analogue scale for instruction adherence; self-vs-other focus VAS (-3 to +3) | Post-conversation self-report |
| Anxiety | Self-rated (VAS) | |
| Social performance | Conversation partner ratings + independent video assessor ratings | |

**CRITICAL OPERATIONALIZATION NOTE**: The "with" and "without" instructions are a *combined* manipulation — they simultaneously manipulate (a) self-focused attention direction, (b) safety behavior use (rehearsing/filtering), and (c) evaluative monitoring. **Cannot isolate safety behavior effects from attentional focus effects.** The study explicitly notes this as a limitation and states the behaviors represent "one particular set."

## Methodology

- **Study Type**: Experimental analogue, within-subjects counterbalanced
- **Sample**: n=57 adolescents, ages 11–14; 28 high anxious / 29 low anxious; split on social anxiety symptoms
- **Measures**: ASBQ (29-item), VAS (anxiety, adherence, focus), partner questionnaire, video observer ratings
- **Duration**: Single session; 2 × 5-minute conversations; naive conversation partners

## Methodological Critique

### Strengths
- First adolescent experimental study — important developmental data
- Within-subjects design with counterbalancing
- Multi-informant: self, partner, observer
- Open access with detailed methods

### Limitations
- SBs and self-focus confounded in the single manipulation
- "One particular set" of SBs: monitoring, rehearsing, filtering — skews toward impression management, may not capture avoidance behaviors (talking less, avoiding eye contact)
- Compliance self-reported only
- n=57 may be underpowered for subgroup analyses
- Adolescent community sample; not clinical

## Relevance to Research Question

Provides verbatim "with" and "without" instruction wording — rare in the literature. Exposes the key operationalization issue: the standard manipulation combines SB elimination with attention direction change, making it impossible to attribute results solely to SBs.

## Connections to Other Sources

- Directly adapted from McManus et al. (2008) adult paradigm
- Instruction wording appears to be the standard format used by the McManus/Clark group
- Cf. PLOS ONE subtypes study (2019) for a version that separately manipulates avoidance vs. impression management
