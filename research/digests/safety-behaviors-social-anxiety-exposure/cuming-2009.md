# Digest: Cuming et al. 2009 — The SAFE (Subtle Avoidance Frequency Examination)

Source: [cuming-2009](../../sources/safety-behaviors-social-anxiety-exposure/cuming-2009.md)
Access: abstract-only
Last Updated: 2026-03-14

## Summary

Development and validation paper for the SAFE (Subtle Avoidance Frequency Examination), a 32-item self-report measure of safety behaviors in social anxiety. Items rated on a frequency scale; three factors emerged. Developed at Macquarie University (Rapee's group).

## Key Findings

- 32-item self-report measure
- Three factors: (1) active "safety" behaviors, (2) subtle restriction of behavior, (3) behaviors aimed at avoiding or concealing physical symptoms
- Strong internal consistency, good construct validity
- Discriminates clinical from non-clinical participants
- Responsive to treatment effects
- Adapted version for speech anxiety: 30 items, 5-point scale from "never" to "always"

| Safety behaviors | 32-item self-report frequency questionnaire | Three factors; frequency-based |

| Subtle behavioral restriction | Factor 2 items | Modifications/limitations to normal behavior |
| Symptom concealment | Factor 3 items: e.g., "speak softly," "avoid eye contact" | Hiding visible anxiety symptoms |
| Construct validity | Correlation with social anxiety measures | Not detailed from abstract |
| Treatment responsiveness | Pre-post comparison in treatment context | Responsive to treatment |

**Example items**: "speaking softly," "avoiding eye contact."

**Operationalization concerns**: Item content for Factors 1 and 2 not available from abstract. The scale appears to measure *frequency* of SB use rather than functional relationship to feared outcomes — a conceptual distinction that matters for understanding what "safety behavior" means in this context.

## Methodology

- **Study Type**: Scale development with factor analysis + validation
- **Sample**: Includes both clinical and non-clinical participants (exact n not available from abstract)
- **Measures**: SAFE (32 items), social anxiety measures for validity
- **Duration**: Not applicable (cross-sectional)

## Methodological Critique

### Strengths
- Validated measure with demonstrated clinical/non-clinical discrimination
- Treatment responsiveness is a strong validity indicator
- Multi-factor structure captures heterogeneity of SBs

### Limitations
- Item content not fully accessible from abstract
- Self-report: doesn't capture SBs that patients are unaware of
- Frequency scale doesn't capture whether the SB is functionally linked to feared outcome (which is the theoretical definition)

## Relevance to Research Question

Key measurement tool for SBs in social anxiety research. Shows that the field has a validated self-report instrument, but its three-factor structure suggests SBs are heterogeneous — raising questions about whether experimental studies that manipulate or eliminate "safety behaviors" are targeting the same construct.

## Connections to Other Sources

- Comparable to SBQ (Clark 2005/Hirsch et al.) used in British studies — two competing instruments
- Similar factor structure to SBQ (Hirsch et al.) but with symptom concealment as a separate factor
