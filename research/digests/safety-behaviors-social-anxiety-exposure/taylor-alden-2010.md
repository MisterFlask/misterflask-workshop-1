# Digest: Taylor & Alden 2010 — Safety Behaviors and Judgmental Biases in SAD

Source: [taylor-alden-2010](../../sources/safety-behaviors-social-anxiety-exposure/taylor-alden-2010.md)
Access: abstract-only
Last Updated: 2026-03-16

## Summary

SAD study (n=80) examining the relationship between impression management safety behaviors and judgmental biases (post-event processing / interpretive bias). Found SB elimination enhanced outcomes. First paper from a two-paper dataset (companion: Taylor & Alden 2011).

## Key Findings

- SB elimination enhanced outcomes in SAD (n=80)
- Impression management SBs examined specifically
- From Blakey & Abramowitz (2016) Table 2: coded as "Eliminating enhanced outcomes"

## Methodology

- **Study Type**: Clinical study (design unknown)
- **Sample**: n=80, SAD; same sample as Taylor & Alden (2011)
- **Measures**: Unknown from abstract

## Relevance to Research Question

Contributes to SAD evidence base showing SB elimination beneficial. The impression management focus is relevant because these SBs may have different interpersonal effects than avoidance SBs.

## Connections to Other Sources

- Taylor & Alden (2011): Same sample, nuanced finding about social approach
- Plasencia et al. (2011): Same research group, different sample
- Blakey & Abramowitz (2016) Table 2: Cited alongside other SAD studies all showing SB elimination beneficial
