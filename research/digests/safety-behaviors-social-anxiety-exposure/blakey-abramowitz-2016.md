# Digest: Blakey & Abramowitz 2016 — Safety Behaviors Review from Inhibitory Learning Perspective

Source: [blakey-abramowitz-2016](../../sources/safety-behaviors-social-anxiety-exposure/blakey-abramowitz-2016.md)
Access: abstract-only
Last Updated: 2026-03-14

## Summary

Major critical review examining SB literature through inhibitory learning lens. Challenges the standard view that SBs are always deleterious in exposure therapy. Notes that findings are "limited, mixed, and controversial." Proposes that SBs interfere with exposure by contextualizing inhibitory associations — making extinction learning context-specific and less generalizable.

## Key Findings

- Definition: SBs = "actions performed to prevent, escape, or minimize feared catastrophes and/or associated distress"
- Research consistently implicates SBs in anxiety maintenance — but *eliminating* them during exposure may not always be necessary
- Inhibitory learning framework: SBs may make extinction context-dependent, reducing generalizability
- Findings across the exposure literature are "limited, mixed, and controversial"
- Discusses availability (SB present but unused) vs. utilization (SB actively used) distinction

## Relevance to Research Question

Key theoretical framing: defines SBs functionally (purpose of preventing feared outcomes), and argues that how they are operationalized in studies matters for interpreting effects. The availability vs. utilization distinction has direct implications for how "banning" SBs should be done.
