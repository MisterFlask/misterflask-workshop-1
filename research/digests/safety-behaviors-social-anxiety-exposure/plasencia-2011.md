# Digest: Plasencia, Alden & Taylor 2011 — Differential Effects of SB Subtypes in SAD

Access: abstract-only
Last Updated: 2026-03-16

## Summary

SAD-specific study (n=93) examining differential effects of avoidance vs. impression management SB subtypes. Found that SBs interfered with outcomes. Provides SAD-domain evidence for SB harm, with subtype differentiation. Full text not yet available.

## Key Findings

- SBs interfered with exposure outcomes (from Blakey & Abramowitz 2016 Table 2 summary)
- Examined two SB subtypes: avoidance and impression management (consistent with SBQ two-factor structure)
- SAD clinical sample (n=93)
