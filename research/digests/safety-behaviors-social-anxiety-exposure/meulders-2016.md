# Digest: Meulders et al. 2016 — Meta-Analysis of Safety Behaviors in Exposure

Source: [meulders-2016](../../sources/safety-behaviors-social-anxiety-exposure/meulders-2016.md)
Access: abstract-only
Last Updated: 2026-03-16

## Summary

Quantitative meta-analysis of the safety behavior during exposure literature (N=23 studies). Aggregate finding: inconclusive — evidence does not support either removal or addition of SBs during exposure. The only meta-analysis directly addressing this question.

## Key Findings

- 23 studies included
- Aggregate result: "inconclusive and could not provide strong evidence supporting either the removal or addition of safety behaviors during exposure" (per citation in Blakey & Abramowitz 2016)
- This null meta-analytic result is consistent with Blakey et al. (2019) RCT null result

- **Study Type**: Meta-analysis
- **Scope**: N=23 studies

## Methodological Critique

Abstract-only — full critique not possible. Key question: Did meta-analysis separate SAD studies from specific phobia studies? If not, the mixed evidence base may average heterogeneous populations.

## Relevance to Research Question

Provides aggregate evidence summary. The "inconclusive" finding mirrors what Blakey & Abramowitz (2016) concluded qualitatively. Together these two reviews from 2016 establish the null/mixed aggregate state of the field before the Blakey 2019 RCT.

## Connections to Other Sources

- Blakey & Abramowitz (2016): Both published 2016, reach same conclusion via different methods
- Blakey et al. (2019): RCT published after both reviews; also null result
- Meulders et al. (2016) is cited by Blakey 2019 as motivation for the RCT
