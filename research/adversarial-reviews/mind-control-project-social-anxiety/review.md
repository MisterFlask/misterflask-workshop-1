## Claim Under Review

The document argues that social anxiety is caused by "the Mind Control Project" -- the attempt to control what other people think of you -- and that the solution is to fully dismantle this project using an OCD-inspired Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) protocol, rather than the standard CBT approach of correcting cognitive distortions.

## Document Summary

This self-help document proposes that social anxiety is structurally identical to OCD, driven by compulsive impression management ("the Mind Control Project"). Rather than correcting distorted beliefs (CBT) or habituating to feared stimuli (traditional exposure), the author argues you should identify and cease all mental and behavioral compulsions aimed at managing others' perceptions, replacing them with principles of Vulnerable Honesty, Kindness, and Selfish Impulse. The author claims anxiety should cease within seconds of genuinely "surrendering" the attempt to control perceptions.

## Round Log

### Round 1: Part 1 -- The Mind Control Project

**Chunk**: #2 -- Part 1 (lines 52-81)

**Pro argued**: The "Mind Control Project" maps precisely to established self-presentational models (Schlenker & Leary 1982, Hofmann 2007, Moscovitch 2009). Clark & Wells CT's 84% recovery rate confirms the causal model because it succeeds by targeting impression management. Behavioral experiments (d=2.59) vastly outperform verbal cognitive restructuring (d=1.02), showing that dismantling the behavioral program is the active ingredient, not correcting beliefs. Biological vulnerability is "soil" but the Project is "what grows in it." Pro concedes C4's "seconds" claim outruns available evidence.

**Con argued**: "Sole cause" claim contradicted by BI (OR=7.59) at 4-14 months, before any capacity for impression management. The document rebrands existing frameworks (Schlenker-Leary) with a catchy name and then overclaims. Modern Clark & Wells CT already targets everything the document proposes -- the document criticizes a stale version of CBT. The "seconds" claim has no empirical support at specified temporal resolution. "Genuine surrender" is an unfalsifiable escape hatch. The "soil" concession is itself an admission of multifactorial causation.

**Evidence found**: Clark et al. 2006 (84% CT recovery); Wells et al. 1995 (SB dropping > exposure alone); 2025 RCT (BE d=2.59 vs verbal CR d=1.02); BI meta-analysis (OR=7.59, 57% non-conversion); 2025 function-centered review (SAD SBs functionally equivalent to OCD compulsions); self-other agreement studies (specific accuracy but global distortion); no data at second-level temporal resolution.

**Cruxes affected**: All four cruxes opened.

**Judge notes**: Both sides made strong arguments. The pro debater's best move was reframing Clark & Wells' success as confirmation of the document's causal model rather than a refutation. The con debater's best move was pointing out that the "soil" concession logically entails multifactorial causation. The pro debater's honest concession on C4 was notable and well-reasoned. The con debater's argument that functional analogy "proves too much" (it would make all anxiety disorders OCD) is a strong logical point that the pro side has not adequately addressed.
