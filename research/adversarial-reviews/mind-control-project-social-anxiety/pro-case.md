# Pro Case: mind-control-project-social-anxiety

## Current Position Summary

The document's core claim -- that social anxiety is caused by compulsive impression management -- is a precise restatement of the Schlenker & Leary (1982) self-presentational model. What the document adds is the claim that anxiety is a *rational signal* from the impression-management system, not a cognitive distortion. This is supported by Hofmann (2007), who found SAD patients set unrealistic social standards and accurately perceive the gap. Moscovitch (2009) argued the core fear in social phobia is self-portrayal concerns -- exactly the "Project." Clark & Wells (1995) identify self-focused attention as consuming cognitive resources, matching the document's resource-depletion claim. Wells et al. (1995) showed that dropping safety behaviors produced significantly greater within-session anxiety reduction than exposure alone -- supporting rapid resolution. The document's critique of CBT (that it reinforces impression management by validating its premises) is logically sound. Biological vulnerability factors (genetics, amygdala) are conceded as predisposing soil, but the Project is what grows in it and produces the suffering.

## Round 1: Part 1 -- The Mind Control Project

### Arguments
- C1 supported by Schlenker & Leary (1982), Hofmann (2007), Moscovitch (2009), Clark & Wells (1995)
- The "Project" is not a fringe rebranding but maps directly to established self-presentational and cognitive models
- Anxiety-as-rational-signal is supported by evidence that SA patients accurately perceive the gap between their impossible standards and likely performance
- Cognitive resource depletion claim is supported by dual-task paradigm studies (Niles et al. 2021)
- Wells et al. (1995) showed rapid within-session anxiety reduction when safety behaviors dropped

### Concessions
- "Directly and solely caused" may overstate exclusivity; biological vulnerability factors exist but operate through the impression-management mechanism
