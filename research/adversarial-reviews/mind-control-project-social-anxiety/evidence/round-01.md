# Evidence Package: Round 01

Chunk: Part 1 -- The Mind Control Project
Requests from: Pro (4 requests), Con (4 requests), merged to 6

## Key Findings Summary

### Request 1: Cognitive Restructuring vs Safety Behavior Elimination
- Clark et al. 2006 RCT: CT (which includes safety behavior dropping) achieved 84% recovery vs 42% for exposure+relaxation
- Wells et al. 1995 (n=8): Exposure + dropping safety behaviors > exposure alone for within-session anxiety reduction
- F-SET RCTs: Brief safety-behavior-focused treatment produced large effect sizes (d=2.01 for anxiety)
- 2025 RCT: Behavioral experiments (d=2.59) outperformed verbal cognitive restructuring (d=1.02)
- GAP: No pure dismantling study isolating safety behavior elimination alone vs cognitive restructuring alone

### Request 2: Are SA Negative Predictions Accurate or Distorted?
- Mixed picture: SA individuals show real performance differences in some contexts (formal speech)
- BUT global self-evaluations are substantially more negative than observer ratings warrant
- Specific behavioral perceptions are more accurate; global/catastrophic interpretations are distorted
- Partial self-fulfilling prophecy: negative beliefs can impair actual performance

### Request 3: Time Course of Anxiety Reduction
- Within-session effects measured at pre/post level (not second-by-second)
- Finest granularity in literature: 5-minute SUDS intervals
- CRITICAL GAP: No study measures anxiety at second-level temporal resolution after dropping safety behaviors
- The "seconds" claim lacks direct empirical support at the specified temporal resolution

### Request 4: OCD vs SA Functional Analysis
- 2025 function-centered review argues SAD safety behaviors are functionally equivalent to OCD compulsions
- DSM-5 separated OCD based on different brain circuits (orbitofrontal/basal ganglia vs amygdala)
- 18% comorbidity; shared repetitive negative thinking but different content
- Debate is genuine and unresolved in the field

### Request 5: Behavioral Inhibition and Biological Factors
- Meta-analysis (Clauss & Blackford 2012, n=877): BI = 7x risk for SAD (OR=7.59)
- 43% of BI children develop SAD vs 12% of controls
- BUT 57% of BI children do NOT develop SAD -- BI is risk factor, not deterministic
- Heritability 13-76% depending on age/method
- BI observable at 4-14 months, well before impression management capacity

### Request 6: Does Modern CBT Already Target What the Document Proposes?
- Clark & Wells CT explicitly targets self-focused attention, safety behaviors, and impression management concerns
- Treatment includes attention retraining, dropping safety behaviors, behavioral experiments from Session 2
- The self-presentation framework (Schlenker & Leary 1982) is already integrated into modern CBT models
- Main potential difference: document frames as single insight vs multi-component protocol
