# Con Case: mind-control-project-social-anxiety

## Current Position Summary

The document's "sole cause" claim is contradicted by substantial biological evidence. Twin studies show ~30-50% heritability for social anxiety. Amygdala hyperreactivity is heritable and measurable in non-clinical populations. Behavioral inhibition -- observable in infants at 4-14 months, before any capacity for impression management -- predicts SAD at 4x the rate. The document conflates a well-established maintaining factor (impression management/safety behaviors) with a sole cause. Clark & Wells themselves embed safety behaviors within a broader model including negative beliefs, anticipatory processing, and vulnerability. The document mischaracterizes CBT: modern Clark & Wells protocol specifically targets self-focused attention and safety behaviors, not "making your Project more successful." The OCD structural identity claim (C2) lacks support: DSM-5 deliberately separated OCD from anxiety disorders based on distinct neurobiology. The "seconds" claim (C4) has no empirical support; even ACT, the closest framework to "surrender," requires 12-15 sessions over weeks. The document provides zero citations, zero sample sizes, zero controlled comparisons -- N=1 self-report.

## Round 1: Part 1 -- The Mind Control Project

### Arguments
- "Sole cause" claim contradicted by ~30-50% heritability (twin studies meta-analysis)
- Amygdala hyperreactivity is heritable, precedes cognitive strategies
- Behavioral inhibition at 4-14 months predicts SAD -- before theory of mind or impression management capacity
- Document conflates maintaining factor with sole cause ("pouring gasoline ≠ starting the fire")
- Mischaracterizes CBT: Clark & Wells protocol already targets self-monitoring and safety behaviors
- DSM-5 separated OCD from anxiety disorders based on distinct neurobiology
- C4 "seconds" claim has no empirical support; ACT requires weeks/months
- Zero empirical citations in the document itself

### Evidence Cited

- Amygdala hyperreactivity (PMC7125356)
- BDNF genetic variation in social anxiety (BBRF)

- DSM-5 OCD reclassification (APA)
- CBT meta-analysis g=0.56 (PMC9834105)
- ACT for SAD trials (PubMed 32321083)

### Concessions
- The document correctly identifies impression management and self-focused attention as important maintaining factors -- this much is well-supported
