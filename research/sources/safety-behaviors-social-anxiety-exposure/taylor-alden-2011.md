## Abstract

Not yet retrieved. Known from Blakey & Abramowitz (2016) Table 2: Same n=80 SAD sample as Taylor & Alden (2010). Finding described as: "Poorer performance but SBs promoted social approach." This is a nuanced finding — SBs were associated with poorer performance quality BUT actually facilitated social approach behavior, contradicting the simple elimination narrative.

## Citation Notes

- Same sample as Taylor & Alden (2010)
- The "SBs promoted social approach" finding is the nuanced piece — relevant to debate about judicious use
- Cited in Blakey & Abramowitz (2016) Table 2 as a SAD study
- The result (SBs → more social approach despite poorer performance) may support Rachman/Milosevic facilitation argument for early treatment
