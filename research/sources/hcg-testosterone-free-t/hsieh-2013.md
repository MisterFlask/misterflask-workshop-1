## Abstract

Purpose: Testosterone replacement therapy may result in azoospermia and potentially infertility. We report on hypogonadal men receiving testosterone replacement and concomitant low dose human chorionic gonadotropin to maintain spermatogenesis.

Materials and Methods: We retrospectively reviewed hypogonadal men receiving testosterone replacement in the form of daily topical gel or weekly intramuscular injection combined with intramuscular human chorionic gonadotropin 500 IU every other day. Semen analyses were obtained at baseline and at followup.

Results: A total of 26 men with a mean age of 35.9 years were included with an average followup of 6.2 months. No differences in semen parameters were observed during greater than 1 year of followup. No impact on semen parameters was observed as a function of testosterone formulation. No patient became azoospermic during concomitant testosterone replacement and human chorionic gonadotropin therapy. Nine of 26 men contributed to pregnancy with their partner during followup. Serum testosterone increased from 207.2 to 1,055.5 ng/dl (p < 0.0001) and free testosterone from 8.1 to 20.4 pg/ml (p = 0.02).

Conclusions: Low dose human chorionic gonadotropin appears effective at maintaining semen parameters in hypogonadal males on testosterone replacement therapy seeking fertility preservation.
