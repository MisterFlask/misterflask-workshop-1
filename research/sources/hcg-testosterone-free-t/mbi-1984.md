## Abstract

Sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) levels are often elevated in sera of patients with testicular insufficiency. It was of interest to determine whether SHBG declines into the normal range after androgen administration. Five normal men and five patients with Klinefelter's syndrome were treated with testosterone enanthate (200 mg, IM every 2 weeks), while an additional five normal men and five patients with hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (HH) were treated with hCG (2000 U, three times a week). Three months after the administration of testosterone or hCG, serum total and free testosterone increased in both normal men and patients. Free testosterone increased significantly in Klinefelter's and HH patients (from 94 +/- 20 and 14 +/- 5 pg/ml to 271 +/- 50 and 276 +/- 41 pg/ml, respectively). The increase in normal men treated with testosterone or hCG was also significant (from 211 +/- 52 and 220 +/- 37 pg/ml to 390 +/- 83 and 330 +/- 90 pg/ml). SHBG fell in both the testosterone-treated normal men (from 6.5 +/- 1.2 to 4.3 +/- 0.4 ng DHT bound/ml) and the testosterone-treated Klinefelter's patients (from 16.4 +/- 2 to 4.3 +/- 0.5). However, SHBG was unchanged in the hCG-treated HH patients and actually rose in the hCG-treated normal men (from 6.6 +/- 0.7 to 8.6 +/- 1.0). We conclude that treatment of hypogonadal men with testosterone and hCG in the doses used increased free testosterone levels above the basal levels for normal men. However, the effects of the increase in free testosterone, as determined by a change in SHBG, were different depending upon the type of treatment.
