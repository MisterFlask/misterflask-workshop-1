Problem

You’ve been thinking about medieval warfare. Specifically, about how castle defenders would hurl rocks and rubble at attackers trying to scale their walls. Everyone talks about boiling oil, but that was actually pretty uncommon; oil was expensive as hell and you don’t waste perfectly good cooking supplies on invaders unless you’re truly desperate. What you do use, is rubble: the broken bits of your own fortifications, the debris and waste stone lying around, turned lethal through elevation and velocity.

You’ve also been thinking about vulnerability. Giving someone the ability to hurt you, really hurt you by opening yourself up to them. Any true expression of desire, you understand, is a vulnerability; it gives someone an angle from which they can destroy you, if they feel like it. Or at least make a pretty good go of it.

This dynamic makes vulnerability feel like, well, a vulnerability: an unsightly, embarrassing pile of psychic rubble you have to keep hidden behind the walls. By logical extension, every conversation must be an exercise in fortification maintenance. Don’t let them see the cracks. Don’t let them know you’re 90% cringe thoughts by volume.

Solution:

You stumbled into a discovery by accident. You were in the middle of destroying yourself socially at a dating workshop — a perfectly reasonable activity — when you noticed something: every piece of mortifying, factual truth you blandly reported on became a precise, armor-piercing round that could punch through someone’s carefully maintained social defenses and make them skip a beat, stutter, forget their lines.

Not performed vulnerability; that’s like going into a gunfight shooting blanks. Not with strangers, either. Real, unprocessed, depleted-uranium cringe with people you’ve met before, with real stakes.

Your deepest mortifications are precision munitions. “Yeah, I actually started doing dance because I wanted to get hot by getting really good at salsa.” BOOM. “Actually I’ve had the smallest of crushes on you for the entire last year, and now I’m happy to have a beautiful container wherein I can act on that.” BANG. “I’ve been fantasizing about making out with you for the last two hours and it’s becoming a problem.” DIRECT HIT.

Watch what happens when you deploy maximum vulnerability with good cheer and zero shame whatsoever: people’s faces do this thing where they buffer, like you’ve submitted input their social processor can’t handle. Most recover and find it charming. Some burst into scandalized laughter. Some flee. But in that moment of detonation, you’ve completely shattered the normal social physics. You’ve created a breach in reality where anything could happen.

And people love it. They WANT you to fire. They’re so used to shields-and-small-talk that when someone starts cheerfully launching vulnerability missiles, it’s the most interesting thing that’s happened to them all week. They lean into the explosion. They want to get hit.

The beautiful thing is, you never run out. Every second of existence generates more cringe. You’re a renewable resource of socio-nuclear material; you have an infinite ammo cheat code for the worst possible weapon. “Oh no, I’m running low on humiliating truths to weaponize!” said no one with a functioning anxiety disorder ever.

You generated so much psychic rubble back when you were trying to control everything. What if you… just started to hurl it in random directions for the sheer love of chaos?
