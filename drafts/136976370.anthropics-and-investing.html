So I’m generally a believer in following propositions:

  • Large language models are a transformative technology that will, as people start to understand how to work with chains of thought and wring genuine reasoning out of a complex and lossy process, revolutionize entire industries.

  • This fact is broadly underappreciated in the world of finance; this being the case, this represents

  • That superhuman AI is possible and, additionally, is an existential threat, for reasons given elsewhere.

As I see it, either LLM technology leads to human extinction/AI ruin, or it doesn’t; and either LLM technology pans out productively, or it doesn’t.
