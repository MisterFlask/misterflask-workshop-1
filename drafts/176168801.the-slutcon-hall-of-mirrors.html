Problem

She made a face. Was it contempt? Amusement? A stray neuron misfiring? She’ll tell you, if you ask, in exhaustive detail.

Welcome to the Hall of Mirrors, just one of many useful services provided by your rather expensive mid-tier SlutCon ticket. Every woman you speak to is a funhouse mirror: warped by her own history, her current mood, whether she ate breakfast, what her last boyfriend was like, the specific memories she associates with the cologne you happen to be wearing.

You’re trying to find your True Face in these mirrors. You peer into one: “He’s being too forward.” You peer into another: “He’s not being forward enough.” A third: “Take me now, stallion of my dreams.” A fourth yawns. You are Schrodinger’s Casanova, simultaneously too much and not enough, bold and cringe, attractive and repulsive, until she observes you and the waveform collapses into... something. That she will tell you about. At length. If you want.

Something inside your skull whispers to you: Okay but what if we just do what ALL the mirrors want? Surely if we optimize for every possible reflection we can become the ULTIMATE LIFEFORM that all the mirrors want to fuck, forever???

No. Absolutely fucking not, that way lies madness and a probable anxiety disorder.

Solution

After some investigation, here’s what you’ve pieced together.

FIRST RULE:

You don’t argue with mirrors.

When a mirror shows you with bed hair, you don’t stand there explaining to the mirror why your hair is actually fine, technically, if you think about it from a certain angle. You can fix your hair or not fix your hair, but the mirror is not playing the judge in the criminal trial of your life. The mirror is not making a moral judgment about your hair that has to be responded to. It’s reflecting photons. That’s the gig. If you’re not interested in hair-related feedback you can stop looking into the mirror, but the photons will be there regardless.

(Note that if you argue with her, and she decides she agrees with you, then you have not actually improved your understanding of how you come off in first impressions. The first impression was over before you opened your mouth.)

SECOND RULE:

One mirror shows you with weird lighting. You might be weird, or the mirror might be weird, or the angle might be weird. Who can say?

Three different mirrors in different locations show you the same thing? Time for some reflection. (That’s a pun. We’re allowed one pun. We’ve been through a lot.)

If every girl you speak to says that you’re giving stalker vibes, the mirrors are achieving consensus. They’re all reflecting the same thing from different angles: something about this behavior is not landing.

This is useful information! This is not about you being fundamentally broken or unloveable. This is: this particular behavior, as filtered through multiple perceptual apparatus, is generating a consistent pattern.

THIRD RULE:

She made a face when you brought up the butt plug thing? The mirror showed you something. You are now informed. You are not obligated.

The mirror’s job is to reflect. YOUR job is to decide what to do with the information.

FOURTH RULE:

You are not obligated to show up well in front of every mirror. Some mirrors will show you as not-sexy. This is not a crisis because your job was never “be attractive in all mirrors.” Your job is “correct any consistent issues you observe in your reflection, and afterwards, find some nice mirrors that reflect you in ways you like and arrange them around yourself in some kind of aesthetically-pleasing way.”

If you tell three different girls the same thing and get three different reactions? Congratulations, you’ve discovered that different girls are different. News at eleven. And this means you can stop trying to find the One True Reflection that will tell you the True Form Of Behavioral Hotness.

Because there is no such reflection, and no mirror will ever show it to you. There are only mirrors, plural, each showing you reflections of varying qualities, and you get to be the one who walks between them, noting what they show but never, ever arguing with them.
