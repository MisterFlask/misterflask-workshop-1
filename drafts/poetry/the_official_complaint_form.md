# The Official Complaint Form

*An Electro Swing Satire*

---

**THE OFFICIAL COMPLAINT FORM**
*(Minneapolis Bureau, January 1943)*

We, the undersigned, humbly complain
Of this Minnesota post—
The temperature's dropped to a ludicrous plane
And we're losing men to the frost.

The Lugers jam up when it's twenty below,
The vehicles refuse to start;
We turn the ignition, we pray, but no go—
The engine's a block of cold art.

The locals give directions with maddening cheer:
"Oh ya, just a few blocks that way!"
We follow their counsel, we trudge through the drear,
And arrive at the selfsame cafe.

There's a system of skyways that spans the whole town,
Glass tunnels with bridges and ramps—
We entered at Hennepin, wandered around,
And emerged at a different camp.

The citizens bundle in scarves to the eyes,
Which renders surveillance absurd;
We can't tell the farmers apart from the spies
When everyone's dressed like a bird.

We've tried interrogation—the natives just shrug:
"That's not too bad," they reply,
"Could be worse," and "Oh sure," with a satisfied mug—
We can't tell compliance from sly!

The leather we're issued does not insulate,
Our overcoats might as well be gauze;
We filed for wool rations—they said "You must wait,"
The supply train is frozen, because.

THEREFORE we request an immediate post
To anywhere else on the map:
The Eastern Front! Africa! Somewhere less gross!
We'd settle for enemy trap.

We await your decision. The radiator's cracked.
Someone has eaten the horse.
Please transfer us out before we are sacked
By this frozen and merciless force.

Respectfully filed,
*[The Frozen Bureau]*
