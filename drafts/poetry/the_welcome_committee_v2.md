# The Welcome Committee (Revised)

*An Electro Swing Song of the Minneapolis Resistance*

Well hello and a welcome to you and your crew!
We just want you to feel right at home while you stay;
There's a few local customs we'd like you to know,
And some tips about life when it's twenty below.

Now the cold in these parts is a thing of its own,
It can crack through your windshield and settle like stone;
And the snow piles up quick, but we'll show you the ropes—
We take care of each other; that's how the North copes.

Now your cars might stop working—the batteries die—
And the street signs go missing; we can't tell you why;
If your tires go flat, well, it's probably the cold,
And the writing on walls? It's just how we stay bold.

You should know there are more of us here than you'd think,
Every window a neighbor, as close as a wink;
When the hard times arrive we don't scatter or flee—
We look after our own; that's the way it should be.

Now the winter is long and the winter is deep,
And by January even the pipes start to weep;
But the folks around here, well, we're built for the cold—
We've been weathering winters since granddad was old.

So we hope you enjoy every day of your stay,
And we'll be right here waiting, whatever you say;
We'll keep waving hello from our side of the street,
And we'll see who's still standing when winter's complete.
