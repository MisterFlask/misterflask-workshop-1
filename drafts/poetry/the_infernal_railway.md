# The Infernal Railway
## A Prospectus in Verse

Come and gather around me, ye men of the pound,
For a prospect more golden was never yet found!
I've journeyed to Hell and I've walked every mile—
And I bring you a scheme that is well worth your while!

From the station at London the railway descends,
Through tunnels that plunge to where daylight all ends,
Through the crust and the mantle, through brimstone and clay—
To the gates of the Pit, where the lost souls all stay!

At the first stop called Limbo the platform is wide,
Where the virtuous pagans in patience abide,
And the babes on the train sit so calm and so still—
Not a cry! Not a whimper! They've quite lost the will!

At Purgatory Junction the passengers change
For the branch lines to torments both subtle and strange,
There's a buffet that serves up a sulfurous tea—
And I tell you, good sirs, it's as fine as can be!

We have tunneled the Alps! We have bridged every sea!
We have spanned the whole globe with our iron decree!
So to bore ourselves down to the furnace below—
Why, it's Progress, dear sirs! It's the way we must go!

At the station called Wrath there is some small unrest,
But the doors are of iron to safeguard each guest!
Let the damned beat the windows and howl as they please—
You'll be snug in your carriage and quite at your ease!

At the terminus Dis you'll behold such a sight!
Vaulted ceilings and arches of brimstone alight!
There's a palace for Satan that towers on high—
And a first-class hotel where investors may lie!

Now the coaches are built with asbestos throughout,
So the flames cannot touch you—of that have no doubt!
There is tinted glass fitted to soften the glare,
And the seats are upholstered with deviling care!

Now consider the profits, my venturing friends!
There's a market below us that never quite ends!
Once a soul has descended it cannot depart—
And the sinners keep coming! Now that's the best part!

If some moralist mutters of brimstone and vice,
I should mention the Bishop who bought in at twice!
His Grace holds a thousand—the Church gives assent!
So invest with a conscience that's perfectly bent!

So step up to the table and put your name down!
It's ten pounds for a share—you won't find one more sound!
As the railway expands and the passengers flow,
You'll be reaping returns from the regions below!

And when finally, gentlemen, your time has come,
And the angels aren't singing and heaven is mum,
You'll discover your ticket was paid in advance—
As a founder, you'll ride down to join in the dance!
