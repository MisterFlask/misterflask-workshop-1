# Saltbush Bill's Cleansing Tonic

*A Patent Medicine Advertisement in Tumbling Alliterative Verse*

---

Now Saltbush Bill was a wandering man, || all weathered and worn and old,
He'd fossicked for fortune and found only flies, || and a future too feeble to hold;
His boots were as busted as Bonaparte's dreams, || his belly was bitter and bare,
When he stumbled upon in a stony ravine, || a hermit with hay-coloured hair.

This gnarled old gaffer was grizzled and grey, || and he grinned with a glittering gaze,
He proffered a potion in purple-stained pot, || and prophesied prosperous days;
"This tincture," he tittered, "is ten thousand years, || from the time of the Pharaohs of old,
It's brewed from the bark of the baobab tree, || and worth twice its weight in gold."

Well, Bill took a belt of the brown bitter brew, || and it burned like a bushfire blaze,
But quick as a quail he was quivering with vim, || and his veins were afire and ablaze!
His bones that were brittle grew brawny and bold, || his sinews sang sudden and strong,
His hair that was halfway to heaven grew thick, || and his teeth that were twisted grew long!

He buried the hermit (who'd breathed his last breath), || and he bottled that beautiful balm,
And Saltbush Bill's Cleansing Tonic was born, || with a cunning and capable calm;
Now he stands in the square with his samples to sell, || and his spiel is a splendid affair—
Step closer, come closer, || you curious crowd! || This cure is beyond all compare!

It cures the catarrh and the canker and croup, || the scurvy, the scrofula too!
It banishes boils and it betters your blood, || and brings back the bloom that you knew!
Got the grippe or the gout or a grumbling gut? || Are you racked by rheumatics and ache?
One spoonful of Saltbush Bill's Sovereign Stuff, || and you'll feel fit and fancy awake!

Are you weary and wasted and worried with woe? || Is your constitution a crime?
Does your liver lie listless, || your lungs like wet leather? || Then NOW is the paramount time!
This wonderful water works wonders on warts, || and will waken the weary to might!
It'll fatten your frame and it'll fire up your flame, || and restore your romantic delight!

It grew hair on a billiard ball, || friends—I have seen it with both of my eyes!
It raised up a rooster that'd rotted a week, || and he crowed at the crack of sunrise!
A cripple from Cootamundra came crawling, || he left doing leaps like a lord!
A widow in Wagga grew twenty years younger, || and married a Methodist's ward!

The Governor-General gargled a glass, || and governed with glorious glee!
A shearer from Shepparton sheared seven thousand, || and still had the strength for his tea!
Professors and parsons and Parliament men, || all praise it from Perth to the Tweed—
There's nothing this nectar of nature can't fix, || no ailment it cannot exceed!

So step right up, friends! || Don't shuffle or shirk! || Just a shilling will set you up right!
Two bob for a bottle of bountiful bliss, || and you'll bless me from morning to night!
The supplies they are scarce—for the secret is rare—|| and tomorrow I'm taking my leave,
So reach for your riches and ready your hands, || and prepare yourself to believe!

For I'm Saltbush Bill, || and I've wandered this world, || from the scrublands to Sydney's great hall,
And I'm telling you truly, || this tonic's tremendous, || the tip-top, the titan of all!
So buy up, buy up! || Ere the bottles are bare! || Let your doubts be demolished today!
For Saltbush Bill's Cleansing Tonic, my friends, || will wash all your worries away!
