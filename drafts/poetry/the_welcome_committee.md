# The Welcome Committee

*An Electro Swing Song of the Minneapolis Resistance*

So you've come to our city with guns and with goons,
In your masks and your uniforms, humming your tunes;
We just want you to know you are welcome to stay—
But we reckon you'll find it quite rough either way.

See, the cold in the North is a creature its own,
It will freeze up your engines and chill you to bone;
We were raised in this weather, we know every trick,
While you huddle for warmth and your rifle-locks click.

Your directions will fail and your street signs will vanish,
There is sugar aplenty for each of your tanks;
And the paint on your walls reads "NO THANKS" to your ranks—
All your tires will flatten, your windows will crack,
And we smile and we wave as we chip at your back.

For there's more of us here than you ever could count,
Every window's a watcher who adds to the mount;
Every face in the crowd is a friend we can trust,
And you can't jail a city—you'd have to take all of us.

Now October is here and you're already cold,
Just wait until January—that's when it gets old;
There's a wind off the prairie that bites through your gear,
And we'll see who is standing come spring of next year.

So enjoy your brief visit, we're glad that you came—
When you leave in the springtime you won't feel the same;
But until that day dawns, we will wave from the street
With a wink and a smile: "Welcome—welcome to defeat!"
