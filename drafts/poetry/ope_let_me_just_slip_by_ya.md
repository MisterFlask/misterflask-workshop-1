# Ope, Let Me Just Slip By Ya

*An electro swing number from occupied Minneapolis*

Oh welcome, dear visitors, we're glad that you're here!
We noticed the boots and the interesting gear.
We hope you'll enjoy all the snow and the ice—
The locals are helpful, we're terribly nice!

Now "ope" is "excuse me" and it's terribly nice,
It's "let me slip past you" or "sorry 'bout the ice."
But ope can mean "gosh, did your paperwork burn?"
Well we hadn't a clue—guess you live and you learn.

Need directions to Uptown? Oh sure, ya betcha!
Take a left at the lake—ope, that ice there won't letcha.
Your patrol should turn right at the third traffic light—
Oh that road's been closed off since the blizzard last night.

Now the sidewalks by headquarters are awfully slick,
And your boys keep on slipping—it happens so quick!
Ope, we don't know what causes that glassy black ice—
We just salt our own walkways, we're terribly nice.

Oh you've got maybe fifty boys guarding each block,
But there's fifty times that keeping eyes on the clock.
When you sleep there are thousands of us still awake—
Ope, who spraypainted "FREEDOM" all over your gate?

Now it's January, friend, and the mercury's dropped,
And your half-tracks are frozen, your tank treads are stopped.
Ope, nobody told you 'bout winter up here?
It's the kind that'll kill you—we have it each year.

You want hot food? The diners are all booked up tight,
And the grocery's sold out—been sold out since last night.
Ope, we'd share our hotdish but there's none to be found—
Guess the tater tots vanished from all over town.

Each morning you wake and the laser loon's there,
Sprayed in red on your walls, on your vehicles, your square.
Ope, we don't know who does it—it happens at night—
But that glowing red loon is a heck of a sight!

So enjoy your stay here, we're so glad that you came!
Every day's an adventure, no two are the same.
Ope, your tires are flat and your heater's gone bust?
Well that's Minneapolis—you just learn to adjust.
