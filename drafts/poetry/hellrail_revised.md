# The HELLRAIL
## A Prospectus in Verse

Ratatat! The prospectus rattles round the ragged rim of reason,
Hear the Hellrail hurtling hellward—Loss of Capital our season!
Gather gold in greedy handfuls, call the broker, sign the scrip,
Stamp the debentures, seal the contract, board the diabolic ship!

Thunderous tenders touted Hellrail, torrid tunnel through the planet's crust,
Contracts copper-bound in codicils and sealed with sulfurous trust;
Engineers in evening tailcoats drafted blueprints by the gaslight's glare,
Diabolic diagrams in chalk and charcoal—pentagrams with flair.

Pistons pounding, boilers belching brimstone, backers braying "Bravo!"
Shares were soaring skyward, silver streamers snapped across the Strand below;
Magnates munching marzipan in marble halls with watered stock in hand,
Widows wheeled their husbands' legacies in barrows, close at hand.

First departure dazzled London: diamond sparks in jets of gold champagne,
Steel rails ringing, red flags flying, roaring crowds acclaim the train;
Pressmen praised the grand project, printing pages packed with pep and pride,
Parsons pocketed their stipends—God and Mammon, side by side.

Then a tremor, then a clangor, then a sulfurous sideways lurch,
Boilerplate began to buckle, brokers' bravado left the church;
Red ink rained on ruined ledgers, rivulets of rancid debt,
Creditors grew clammy, crawling round the chief clerk's desk in sweat.

Demons drafted legal dunning, writs in brimstone, bonds in blood,
Solicitors in sulfur wigs served papers for the hellfire flood;
Interest compounded hourly on the infernal lending rate,
Underwriters ulcerated, overdrawn and overdue—too late!

Shareholders in sheer hysterics sold at sixpence on the pound,
Court convened in caverns candleblack, the gavel's granite sound;
Demon barristers in horsehair wigs read charges from a scroll:
"Bankruptcy in perpetuity, with interest on the soul."

A memo came from Pandemonium: the Hellrail had been sold,
Absorbed by a larger demon—Gehenna Holdings, I am told;
My shares were diluted down to nothing in the grand restructure,
The board expressed regret, of course, and thanked me for my venture.

Now I stand on sooty sidings, selling matches in the rain,
And sometimes in the night I hear the whistle of the train;
It thunders past with fresh investors, champagne flutes raised high and new—
But Hell has lost my file. I'm not worth damning. Nothing's due.
