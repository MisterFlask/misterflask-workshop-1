There is a strand of thought in the technolibertarian community that the best AI is open-sourced and downloadable by anyone. “Safetyism” is a word said with a sneer; surely (we are told) the only safe AI is one accessible to everyone, equally, in the form of open-weights models that any one government cannot prevent anyone from accessing or modifying?

I’d like to discuss this proposition a bit further. In the form of thought experiments!

A Gun In Every Cabinet

Suppose every American had a gun. If you ask Japanese schoolchildren (I am told) then this is already presumed true.

That means every American can kill every other American. Which hasn’t devolved into a Hobbesian war of all-against-all because… well, just because you can kill someone doesn’t mean things will go well for you in the aftermath. The fact of guns existing doesn’t mean the state doesn’t have a monopoly on violence.

Sometimes people still try to kill other people with guns; but more often than not, this results in the shooter either getting killed or imprisoned. For all but the unlucky victims, life goes on.

A Ring of Invisibility On Every Hand

Let’s now crank this down a notch.

What if the ingredients for a ring of invisibility were a known technology? As hard to make as a telephone.

Guns make it easy to commit violence; rings of invisibility make it easier to get away with violence.
